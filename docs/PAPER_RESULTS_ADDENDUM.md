## SPY buy-and-hold benchmark

The baseline is now SPY positive-month rate, not an always-up classifier. In the generated validation panel, SPY was positive in **53.2%** of months. The honest interpretation is that HFFI is stronger as a household fragility and suitability signal than as a standalone market-timing signal unless a model consistently exceeds this base rate.

## Recommendation backtest

For each household-month, the system selects one market using household HFFI, debt service, liquidity buffer, macro regime, market momentum, volatility, drawdown, and safety. A comparison portfolio randomly ignores the HFFI-conditional recommendation.

| Strategy | Avg 12m return proxy | Avg drawdown proxy | 10th percentile drawdown | n |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Followed HFFI recommendation | 90.91% | -3.65% | -10.09% | 3,120 |
| Ignored random alternative | -16.50% | -16.02% | -31.19% | 3,120 |

This is the defense chart: following HFFI-conditional recommendations produces materially lower realized drawdown proxies in synthetic stress regimes, especially around 2020 and 2022-like periods.

## Plain-language recommender claims supported in the code

- Every household receives one selected market, not a random spread across all markets.
- Stable households can receive higher-growth recommendations; stretched households are shifted toward resilience.
- Recommendation scores fall as HFFI, debt service, market volatility, and drawdown risk rise.
- During risk-off macro regimes, the scoring weights shift toward safety and away from raw returns.
- A deterministic test case with HFFI ≈ 42 selects Commodities as the Moderate-risk recommendation.

## SCF calibration hook
